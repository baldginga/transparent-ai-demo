The problem

When algorithms decide,
who can you argue with?

The Australian Robodebt scheme illegally raised debts against welfare recipients using automated income averaging — a flawed methodology buried in code, invisible to the people it harmed. The UK Post Office Horizon scandal convicted hundreds of subpostmasters based on accounting software errors that no one could examine. In both cases, people faced decisions with no visible reasoning and no meaningful path to challenge them. AI language models offer a fundamentally different approach.

Opaque rule engines
Traditional automated systems apply eligibility rules through code that people cannot read. When a decision is wrong, there is no log, no stated reasoning, and no specific point to contest.
AI with a paper trail
A language model working through an eligibility decision produces its reasoning in plain English. Every inference is stated. Every uncertainty is flagged.

How it works

Transparent Governance Architecture

1
Structured Context
Applicant inputs are processed against real-world 2026 legislation rates and abatement logic without hidden black-box calculations.
2
Schema-Guided Generation
Gemini is required to produce explicit, step-by-step reasoning for each condition before outputting a final decision.
3
Auditable Receipts
The generated receipt guarantees that every step of logic can be individually contested or overridden by human reviewers.
